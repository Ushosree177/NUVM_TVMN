# TVMN Shape Analysis Observations

This file records observations only for the requested shape-analysis stage.

## PDF Shape

- TVMN densities are symmetric around zero because only the variance is mixed and E[X | V]=0.
- Smaller variance intervals concentrate probability near zero and produce a sharper peak.
- Larger maximum variance b increases tail thickness because more mass is assigned to larger conditional variances.
- Moving the mode m toward larger values shifts more mixing weight toward larger variances, flattening the center and lifting the tails.

## Tail Study

- The [-5, 5] view shows central peakedness and moderate-tail behavior.
- The [-10, 10] view makes the far-tail differences clearer; parameter sets with larger b retain visibly higher tail density.

## TVMN vs Normal

- Base TVMN parameters: a=0.5, m=1.0, b=2.0.
- Matched Normal comparator: N(0, 1.166667), with standard deviation 1.080123.
- The TVMN curve can be more peaked near zero and heavier in the tails than the variance-matched Normal because it is a variance mixture.

## TVMN vs NUVM

- NUVM is the uniform variance mixture predecessor; TVMN replaces the uniform variance law with a triangular variance law.
- In the comparison plot, TVMN and NUVM are matched by unconditional mean and variance.
- TVMN gives extra control through m, the most likely variance, which NUVM does not have.

## Simulation Histogram

- The simulated sample is generated by first drawing V from the triangular variance distribution and then drawing X from N(0,V).
- The histogram aligns with the theoretical PDF, supporting the random-generation algorithm.
